Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, students will be able to:

Describe different auction formats (e.g., first-price, second-price, English, Dutch), their rules, and underlying assumptions.

Model resource allocation settings as auctions and identify the conditions under which each auction type is appropriate.

Analyse bidder strategies and equilibrium concepts in auctions.

Apply mathematical and algorithmic methods to compute equilibrium bids and expected outcomes in simple auction settings.

Evaluate the efficiency, revenue, and vulnerability to manipulation for various auction mechanisms.

Compare the strategic properties and economic performance of alternative auction formats in theory and in application.

Discuss applications of auction theory in economics, computer science, marketplaces, and multi-agent systems.
